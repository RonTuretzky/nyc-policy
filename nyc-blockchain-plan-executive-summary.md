# NYC Blockchain Plan - Executive Summary

**Document Date:** December 2025
**Issuing Authority:** NYC Office of Technology and Innovation (OTI)

---

## Overview

The NYC Blockchain Plan is a first-of-its-kind municipal framework for the responsible exploration and deployment of blockchain technology across New York City government. Released by Mayor Eric Adams and CTO Matt Fraser, the plan establishes a structured approach to evaluating blockchain use cases while acknowledging both the technology's potential and its limitations.

The plan examines **more than 100 public-sector use cases** and policy interventions, emphasizing that blockchain is not a one-size-fits-all solution but is most promising where multiple parties must share verifiable records and automation is beneficial.

---

## Core Philosophy

> "The opportunity lies not in adopting blockchain for its own sake, but in creating the structures to responsibly pilot, evaluate, and deploy it where it can genuinely improve government services and enhance equity, accountability, and public trust."

**Key Principles:**
- **Fit-for-purpose, not first-to-adopt** — Blockchain implementation should solve real problems, not chase trends
- **Responsible innovation** — Careful evaluation of trade-offs before deployment
- **Public benefit focus** — Improving government services and public trust

---

## The Blockchain Trilemma

The plan explicitly addresses the **blockchain trilemma** — the inherent trade-offs between:

| Factor | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Security** | Protection against tampering and unauthorized access |
| **Privacy** | Control over who can view transaction data |
| **Decentralization** | Distribution of control across multiple parties |

The plan acknowledges these trade-offs are real: *"Choices among decentralization, security, privacy, scalability, and cost drive whether an implementation is viable in government. These trade-offs must be carefully weighed against each other."*

---

## Blockchain Mechanisms & Benefits

### Core Technical Properties

1. **Tamper-Evident Design**
   - Records cannot be altered without detection
   - Strengthens trust and accountability
   - Provides auditable history of all changes

2. **Distributed Ledger Architecture**
   - All participants access the same information simultaneously
   - Supports transparency across agencies and stakeholders
   - Enables collaboration without depending on a single authority

3. **Automation Capabilities**
   - Reduces manual verification work
   - Streamlines reconciliation processes
   - Decreases paperwork and administrative overhead
   - Potential for cost reduction

4. **Smart Contracts** (referenced in related NY State legislation)
   - Self-executing contracts with terms written in code
   - Automatic enforcement of agreement conditions
   - NY State Assembly Bill A8103 proposes legal recognition for blockchain-secured signatures and smart contracts

- Grow economic opportunities for New Yorkers
- Attract world-class talent and investment

**Key Activities:**
- Form advisory commission of blockchain industry leaders
- Evaluate blockchain use cases with city agencies
- Collaborate with federal and state agencies on education and consumer protection

### NYC Blockchain Interagency Working Group

**Purpose:**
- Coordinate standards across city agencies
- Share lessons learned from pilots
- Align on technical and policy requirements
- Facilitate knowledge transfer

## Pilot Programs & Use Cases

### First Pilot: Asbestos Certification Verification

**Agencies Involved:** OTI + NYC Department of Environmental Protection (DEP)
**Objective:** Test blockchain for verifying asbestos handler certifications

This pilot explores using blockchain to create an immutable, verifiable record of asbestos certification status, reducing fraud potential and streamlining compliance verification.

### Evaluated Use Cases Under Consideration

| Category | Potential Applications |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Vital Records** | Birth certificates, death records |
| **Permitting** | Construction permits, business licenses |
| **Data Sharing** | Secure inter-agency data exchange |
| **Public Records** | Property deeds, legal documents |
| **Payment Systems** | Cryptocurrency for taxes and municipal fees (under exploration) |

## Digital Asset Initiatives

### Cryptocurrency Payment Exploration

The city is exploring whether residents could use digital currencies to pay:
- Taxes
- Municipal service fees
- Other city-related payments

This follows similar initiatives in cities like Detroit and states like Colorado.

### Financial Inclusion Goals

Priority focus on serving **unbanked and underbanked communities** by:
- Supporting safer ways to save money
- Improving access to financial resources
- Building resilience against inflation
- Expanding payment options

## Risk Considerations

The plan acknowledges blockchain applications introduce certain risks:

| Risk Category | Considerations |
|---------------|----------------|
| **Privacy** | Data exposure, right to be forgotten compliance |
| **Data Security** | Key management, access control |
| **Systems Integration** | Legacy system compatibility, interoperability |
| **Cost** | Implementation, maintenance, and operational expenses |
| **Environmental Impact** | Energy consumption of certain blockchain types |

---

## Implementation Approach

### Evaluation Framework

1. **Identify Problem** — Is there a real problem blockchain can solve?
2. **Assess Fit** — Does the use case require multi-party verification and transparency?
3. **Analyze Trade-offs** — Security vs. privacy vs. decentralization
4. **Pilot** — Small-scale testing with defined success metrics
5. **Evaluate** — Measure outcomes against objectives
6. **Scale or Adjust** — Expand successful pilots or iterate on approach

### Success Criteria

- Improved accountability
- Reduced manual work
- Lower operational costs
- Enhanced public trust
- Better inter-agency collaboration

## Related Policy & Legislation

### Executive Order 57 (October 14, 2025)
- Establishes Office of Digital Assets and Blockchain
- Directs immediate operations
- Tasks office with industry growth and public education

### NY State Assembly Bill A8103
- Recognizes blockchain-secured signatures as valid electronic signatures
- Allows smart contracts to exist in commerce
- Ensures contracts with smart contract terms cannot be denied legal effect solely due to blockchain implementation
