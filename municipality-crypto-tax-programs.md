# Municipality and State-Based Cryptocurrency Tax Payment Programs

A comprehensive analysis of government programs accepting cryptocurrency for tax payments and municipal services, including cost estimates, usage data, and verification sources.

---

## Executive Summary

As of late 2024, a small but growing number of U.S. states and municipalities, along with several Swiss cantons and cities, accept cryptocurrency for tax payments and government services. Adoption remains extremely limited, with most programs seeing minimal usage despite significant media attention.

**Key Finding:** Programs that have published usage data show negligible adoption. Colorado, the pioneer in state-level crypto tax payments, received only 78 payments totaling ~$57,000 over three years (2022-2024), representing 0.0005% of state income tax revenue.

## United States Programs

### State-Level Programs

#### 1. Colorado (Active - Best Documented)

**Status:** Active since September 2022
**Rank:** First U.S. state to accept crypto for all state taxes

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Launch Date** | September 1, 2022 |
| **Taxes Covered** | All state taxes (income, business, sales, use, severance, excise) |
| **Payment Processor** | PayPal Cryptocurrencies Hub |
| **Fees** | $1.00 + 1.83% of payment |
| **Cryptocurrencies** | Bitcoin, Ethereum, Litecoin, Bitcoin Cash |

**Usage Statistics:**

| Year | Number of Payments | Total Amount |
|------|-------------------|--------------|
| 2022 (Sept-Dec) | 8 | $16,426 |
| 2023 | 22 | $23,241 |
| 2024 | 48 | $17,544 |
| **Total** | **78** | **~$57,000** |

**Context:** Colorado collected $11 billion in individual income tax in FY 2022-23 alone. The total crypto payments represent just 0.0005% of that figure.

**Why Low Adoption:**
- Economic disincentive (better rates on exchanges)
- Potential capital gains tax triggered by spending crypto
- Strong crypto returns discourage spending

**Official Source:** [Colorado Department of Revenue - Cryptocurrency](https://tax.colorado.gov/cryptocurrency)

#### 4. Ohio (Suspended/Revived)

**Status:** Original program suspended (2019); new authorization approved (September 2025)

**Original OhioCrypto.com Program (2018-2019):**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Launch Date** | November 2018 |
| **Suspension Date** | October 2019 |
| **Taxes Covered** | 23 types of business taxes only |
| **Payment Processor** | BitPay |
| **Fees** | 1% (vs. 2.5% for credit cards) |
| **Cryptocurrencies** | Bitcoin only |

**Why Suspended:**
- Legal and procedural concerns raised by incoming Treasurer Robert Sprague
- Questions about whether BitPay qualified as a "financial transaction device" under Ohio law

**2025 Revival:**
- Ohio State Board of Deposit unanimously approved cryptocurrency acceptance (May 2025)
- Ohio House passed "Ohio Blockchain Basics Act" (H.B. 116) in June 2025
- Prohibits local governments from restricting cryptocurrency use
- Exempts small transactions (under $200) from capital gains tax reporting

**Sources:**
- [Ohio Treasurer OhioCrypto.com Update (PDF)](https://tos.ohio.gov/files/Pdfs/Crypto/10%2002%202019%20Crypto%20One-Pager.pdf)
- [Ohio Secretary of State Press Release](https://www.ohiosos.gov/media-center/press-releases/2025/2025-04-24/)

## International Programs

### Switzerland (Global Leader)

Switzerland is the global leader in government cryptocurrency acceptance, with multiple cantons and cities offering crypto tax payments since 2016.

#### 8. Canton of Zug (Active - Pioneer)

**Status:** Active since 2016 (taxes since February 2021)
**Rank:** First jurisdiction worldwide to accept Bitcoin for government services (2016)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Launch Date** | 2016 (services); February 2021 (full taxes) |
| **Services Covered** | All cantonal taxes (income, wealth, federal, inheritance, gift) |
| **Payment Processor** | Bitcoin Suisse AG |
| **Fees** | 1% |
| **Payment Limit** | CHF 1,500,000 (~$1.7M USD) - increased from CHF 100,000 in March 2023 |
| **Cryptocurrencies** | Bitcoin (BTC), Ethereum (ETH) |

**How It Works:**
- Taxpayers scan QR code on invoice
- Pay via mobile wallet
- Bitcoin Suisse converts to CHF instantly
- Canton receives Swiss Francs only (no crypto exposure)

**Requirements:**
- Smartphone/tablet with camera
- Mobile crypto wallet
- Entire invoice must be paid in one cryptocurrency

**Official Source:** [Canton of Zug - Tax Payments with Cryptocurrencies](https://zg.ch/de/steuern-finanzen/steuern/steuerbezug/taxpaymentswithcryptocurrencies)

## Key Findings

### Why Adoption Remains Low

1. **Economic Disincentive:** Exchange fees (1-2%) are higher than free ACH transfers
2. **Capital Gains Tax:** Spending crypto triggers taxable events in most jurisdictions
3. **Opportunity Cost:** Strong crypto returns discourage spending
4. **Holder Mentality:** Most view crypto as long-term investment, not payment method
5. **Limited Awareness:** Many taxpayers unaware of the option

### Best-Performing Programs

**By Adoption:**
1. **Lugano, Switzerland** - Most comprehensive (360+ merchants, 100% city dues)
2. **Zug, Switzerland** - Highest limits (CHF 1.5M), longest history
3. **Colorado** - Highest documented U.S. adoption (78 payments)

**By Cost Efficiency:**
1. **BitPay programs** (Williston, former Ohio) - 1% flat fee
2. **Swiss programs** - 1% fee, no transaction fee
3. **PayPal programs** - $1 + 1.83% (higher for small payments)

### Recommended Models

For jurisdictions considering implementation:

1. **High-Value Payments:** Follow Zug model (Bitcoin Suisse, high limits)
2. **Broad Adoption:** Follow Lugano model (merchant integration + taxes)
3. **Low Cost Entry:** Follow Williston model (BitPay, limited scope initially)
4. **Maximum Accessibility:** Follow Detroit model (PayPal, familiar interface)
