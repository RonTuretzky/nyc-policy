Overview

What is the Use Case Lab?

An applied research initiative at the Ethereum Foundation focused on high-impact use cases through collaborative prototyping, pilots, and standards.

The Verifiable Cities track examines where coordination and verifiability constraints are most acute in urban systems, and where public blockchains can meaningfully expand civic and state capacity.

Core Thesis

Rather than introduce new centralized platforms, blockchain can embed transparency, accountability, and coordination capabilities directly into how public rules and finances operate. This creates "credibility environments" around government claims—a shared digital "trust layer" that extends accountability to transparent technical mechanisms.

Strategic Rationale

Why Cities?

Four hypotheses for why municipalities are the optimal level for blockchain-enabled public infrastructure.

1

Proximity

Cities are the primary institutional level where coordination and service delivery must happen in real time, with proximity to concerned residents.

2

Pragmatism

Cities face immediate service demands across dense networks of public and private actors, requiring practical—not theoretical—solutions.

3

Replicability

Thousands of municipalities worldwide face common operational needs, creating underleveraged opportunities for experimentation and knowledge transfer.

4

Leverage

Cities are powerful levers for public sector innovation, offering a scalable pathway for modernizing civic and state capacity.

Important Note

While Swiss programs demonstrate government crypto acceptance, usage data shows minimal adoption (similar to US state programs). The Verifiable Cities approach focuses on verification and coordination use cases with clearer value propositions than payment acceptance.
