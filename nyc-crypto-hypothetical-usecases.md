## Detailed Use Cases

### Hypothetical Refactorings (from existing programs)

#### 1. Public Ownership of Blockchain Infrastructure

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Current State** | NYC Blockchain Center operated by VC affiliates (Future Perfect Ventures, GBBC Labs) |
| **Proposed State** | Municipalize as public utility with community governance |
| **Governance Structure** | Board: 2 city agency reps, 2 CUNY faculty, 2 labor union reps, 2 community org reps, 1 public interest tech rep |
| **Key Requirements** | All code open source and public domain; no proprietary vendor lock-in; mission focused on public interest |
| **Political Coalition** | Tech Workers Coalition NYC, tenant organizations, union locals |
| **Legislative Path** | City Council legislation requiring public interest governance for publicly-funded tech infrastructure |
| **Oversight Mechanism** | Comptroller audit of existing contracts and funding flows |

---

#### 2. Community-Controlled Digital Wallets

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Current State** | City-controlled Digital Wallet tracking food purchases, potential law enforcement data sharing |
| **Proposed State** | Public benefit corporation with community governance |
| **Privacy Protections** | Data minimization; explicit ban on law enforcement/ICE data sharing; no behavioral tracking |
| **User Rights** | User-controlled data portability and deletion; consent-based sharing |
| **Governance Structure** | Community oversight board with majority representation from benefit recipients |
| **Political Coalition** | Legal services organizations, privacy advocates, immigrant rights groups |
| **Legislative Path** | City Council legislation creating Digital Wallet Public Benefit Corporation |
| **Oversight Mechanism** | Privacy Impact Assessment requirement; Comptroller audit authority over data practices |

---

#### 3. Worker-Owned Remittance Cooperative

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Current State** | City promotes private crypto companies (Bitso, Strike) for remittance services |
| **Proposed State** | Worker-owned remittance cooperative owned by immigrant workers who use it |
| **Revenue Model** | Revenue above operating costs returns to members or funds community development |
| **Technical Infrastructure** | Based on NYC Node program (CUNY-developed); integration with Public Bank NYC proposals |
| **Language Support** | Multilingual: Spanish, Chinese, Bengali, Haitian Creole |
| **Political Coalition** | Make the Road NY, DRUM, Adhikaar, immigrant worker centers |
| **Legislative Path** | Partner with Worker Cooperative Business Development Initiative (NYC SBS); seed funding via NYCEDC |
| **Connection to Broader Agenda** | Links to Public Bank NYC campaign for financial infrastructure |

---

#### 4. Tenant Power Blockchain

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Current State** | BigApps Real Estate challenge focused on "asset management" (landlord interests) |
| **Proposed State** | Tenant-controlled documentation system |
| **Features** | Rent payment history (tenant-verified); repair requests with timestamps/photos; housing code violations; harassment documentation; lease terms/deadlines |
| **Integration** | Connected to 311 complaint system; data accessible to Housing Court, HPD, tenant attorneys |
| **Key Protection** | Landlord cannot alter or delete tenant-submitted records |
| **Political Coalition** | Crown Heights Tenant Union, Met Council on Housing |
| **Legislative Path** | Housing Committee legislation (Council Members Chi Ossé, Alexa Avilés) |
| **Pilot Programs** | HPD and Housing Court pilots; integration with Right to Counsel implementation |

---

#### 5. Participatory Budgeting Enhancement

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Current State** | No blockchain applications for democratic participation in NYC |
| **Proposed State** | Blockchain-based participatory budgeting system |
| **Features** | Transparent, tamper-proof vote counting; real-time tracking of funded project implementation; community proposal submission and deliberation tools |
| **Integration** | Integration with existing PB process in City Council districts |
| **Scale Potential** | Pilot for larger capital budget allocation |
| **Political Coalition** | Participatory Budgeting Project |
| **Legislative Path** | Pilot in progressive Council Member districts; scale based on demonstrated legitimacy improvements |
| **Connection to Broader Agenda** | Broader democratization of budget process |

---

#### 6. Public Bank Integration

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Current State** | No connection between crypto initiatives and public banking movement |
| **Proposed State** | NYC Public Bank incorporates blockchain for transparency and governance |
| **Blockchain Uses** | Transparent reserve management; community investment tracking; democratic governance of lending decisions; integration with worker cooperative support; affordable housing finance tracking |
| **Alternative to BitBonds** | Municipal bonds with transparent blockchain-tracked deployment to affordable housing, public transit, green infrastructure |
| **Political Coalition** | Public Bank NYC campaign, ANHD, NYCETC, local CDFIs |
| **Legislative Path** | State legislation enabling municipal banking; Comptroller advocacy (as future bank commissioner) |
| **Governance** | Community investment coalition governance |

## Alternative Governance Frameworks

| Framework | Principle | Structure | Precedent |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Municipal Digital Commons** | Blockchain as public utility | NYC Digital Commons Authority (PBC); Board: 50% community, 30% agencies, 20% experts; cost recovery only; universal service obligation | Municipal broadband (Chattanooga EPB), public power utilities |
| **Solidarity Technology Cooperative** | Tech serving solidarity economy owned by solidarity economy | Coop federation of worker coops, CLTs, mutual aid; platform coop model; one member one vote; revenue sharing | Mondragon cooperative network, Stocksy United |
| **Community Data Trust** | Data governed by those who generate it | Fiduciary duty to residents; consent-based sharing; revenue returns to community; community veto over harmful uses | Data trusts movement, indigenous data sovereignty |
| **Democratic Technology Assessment** | Communities decide on tech deployment | Mandatory community impact assessment; right to reject; ongoing monitoring; sunset clauses | Environmental impact assessment, CBAs |
